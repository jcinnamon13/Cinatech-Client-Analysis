SECTION 3: TARGET MARKET

Ideal Client Profile:
My ideal clients are working professionals aged 28-45 in desk-based roles — software engineers, project managers, lawyers, and finance professionals. They typically present with chronic lower back pain or neck pain that has been affecting their productivity and sleep for 6-18 months. They have tried generic advice (stretching, standing desks) without sustained relief. They want a structured, expert-led solution, not a gym membership or generic physiotherapy.

What my clients are trying to achieve:
They want to get through a full working day without pain so they can perform at their job, be present at home, and stop worrying about whether their body is going to get worse. Most are not athletic — they are not trying to run a marathon. They want functional normality.

What makes them choose me over other chiropractors:
- I specialise in desk worker injuries specifically, which resonates immediately with this audience
- I explain the biomechanical cause of their problem in detail before treating — most competitors just treat without explaining
- I offer a structured 12-session programme with clear progress milestones rather than open-ended repeat visits

SECTION 6: ONBOARDING AND DELIVERY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Current client onboarding process:
1. New client contacts me via website form or phone
2. I call them back personally within 24 hours (always me — I have no front desk staff)
3. I conduct a 15-minute discovery call to assess fit
4. I send a manual email confirmation with intake forms attached as PDFs
5. First appointment — I conduct a full assessment (45 min) and recommend a programme or single sessions

Biggest operational challenges:
- I am the single point of contact for every client at every stage. When I am in clinic seeing clients, I cannot respond to enquiries, which means some leads go cold.
- I have no CRM. I track client progress in a paper notes system and a basic spreadsheet.
- I have no automated follow-up after a client completes their programme. If they relapse 6 months later, there is nothing in place to bring them back.
